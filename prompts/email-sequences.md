## 1. Product Launch Sequence (5 Emails)

**Goal:** Build anticipation, launch, close with urgency
**Best for:** Digital products, courses, tools, SaaS launches

### Email 1: The Problem (Day -3)

**Subject:** `[PAIN POINT] is costing you more than you think`

```
Hey [NAME],

Quick question: how much time did you spend on [PAINFUL TASK] this week?

If you're like most [AUDIENCE], the answer is [RELATABLE NUMBER].
That's [CALCULATED ANNUAL COST IN TIME/MONEY] over a year.

I know because I tracked it myself. For [TIME PERIOD], I logged every
hour I spent on [TASK]. The number was embarrassing.

So I started building something to fix it.

More on that soon. For now, I want you to notice something this week:
every time you [PAINFUL TASK], write down how long it takes.

The numbers will surprise you.

Talk soon,
[NAME]
```

**Notes:** No product mention. Pure problem awareness. Plant the seed.

## 2. Welcome / Nurture Sequence (4 Emails)

**Goal:** Turn a new subscriber into a fan who opens every email
**Best for:** Newsletter onboarding, lead magnet follow-up, community building

**Subject:** `Here's your [LEAD MAGNET] + one thing most people miss`

```

Welcome. Here's what you signed up for:

[CTA BUTTON: Download [LEAD MAGNET]]

While you're here -- let me tell you what this list is actually about.

I send [FREQUENCY] emails about [TOPIC]. No fluff, no 2,000-word
essays to say one thing. Just [SPECIFIC VALUE PROPOSITION].

Here's what to expect:
- [CONTENT TYPE 1] every [FREQUENCY]
- [CONTENT TYPE 2] when I find something worth sharing
- Occasional recommendations for tools that I personally use

One thing I'd ask: move this email to your Primary tab and reply
with the word "[WORD]." It tells Gmail we're friends, and you won't
miss future emails.

Talk soon,
[NAME]
```

## 3. Re-Engagement Sequence (3 Emails)

**Goal:** Win back subscribers who stopped opening emails
**Best for:** List hygiene, reactivation campaigns

### Email 1: The Honest Check-In (Day 0)

**Subject:** `Still interested in [TOPIC]?`

```
Hey [NAME],

I noticed you haven't opened an email from me in a while.

No guilt trip. People's interests change. Inboxes overflow.
I get it.

But I don't want to keep emailing someone who'd rather I didn't.

So here's the deal:

If you still want [VALUE YOU PROVIDE], click below and I'll keep
sending you [CONTENT TYPE]:

[CTA BUTTON: Yes, Keep Sending]

If not, no action needed. I'll remove you from the list in [DAYS]
days so your inbox stays clean.

Either way, I appreciate you being here.

[NAME]
```

---

### Email 2: The Value Reminder (Day +3)

**Subject:** `In case you missed it: [BEST RECENT CONTENT]`

```
Hey [NAME],

Before I clean up my list, I wanted to make sure you saw this:

[LINK TO BEST RECENT CONTENT]

It's about [TOPIC] and [NUMBER] people found it useful.

Also been working on:
- [RECENT THING 1]
- [RECENT THING 2]
- [UPCOMING THING]

If any of this is relevant to you, stick around:

[CTA BUTTON: I Want to Stay]

If not, this is my second-to-last email to you. No hard feelings.

[NAME]
```

---

### Email 3: The Clean Break (Day +7)

**Subject:** `Removing you tomorrow (unless...)`

```
Hey [NAME],

Last email.

Tomorrow I'm cleaning my list, and if you haven't clicked to stay,
I'll remove your address. You won't hear from me again.

If you want to keep getting [VALUE]:

[CTA BUTTON: Keep Me On the List]

If not, I genuinely wish you well. Thanks for being here.

[NAME]

P.S. -- You can always re-subscribe at [WEBSITE] if you change
your mind later.
```

## Email Writing Rules

### Subject Lines

| Pattern | Example | Best For |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Curiosity gap | "The tool I almost didn't share" | Open rate |
| Direct benefit | "Get 3x more replies with this template" | Conversion |
| Personal | "Quick question, [NAME]" | Re-engagement |
| Urgency | "12 hours left" | Deadline-driven |
| Social proof | "[NAME] just hit $10K using this" | Trust building |

### Body Copy Rules

1. **One idea per email** -- if you need more, send another email
2. **One CTA per email** -- multiple links confuse and reduce clicks
3. **Write at 8th grade reading level** -- short sentences, simple words
4. **Use line breaks liberally** -- dense paragraphs kill mobile readability
5. **Every email should be valuable even without a purchase** -- pure pitch emails erode trust

### Timing

| Sequence Type | Best Send Time |
|--------------|----------------|
| Launch | Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM local |
| Welcome | Immediately on signup |
| Re-engagement | Tuesday or Wednesday, 9 AM local |
| Abandoned cart | 1 hour, then +1 day, then +3 days |
| Referral | After a positive interaction or milestone |

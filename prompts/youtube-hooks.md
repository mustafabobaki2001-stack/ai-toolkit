# YouTube Hook Formulas

15 scroll-stopping openers reverse-engineered from videos with 1M+ views. The first 2 seconds decide if someone stays or swipes. These formulas are designed to interrupt the scroll and lock attention.

Replace `[BRACKETED]` placeholders with your specific topic.

---

## 1. The Insider Secret

**Retention trigger:** Curiosity gap + exclusivity

```
Nobody is talking about this, and honestly, I understand why.
[TOPIC] just changed permanently -- and most people have no idea.
```

**Why it works:** Implies the viewer is about to learn something the mainstream missed. The "I understand why" adds intrigue -- why would people avoid this topic?

---

## 2. The Pattern Interrupt

**Retention trigger:** Direct command + time scarcity

```
Stop scrolling. I need 60 seconds.
What I'm about to show you about [TOPIC] took me [TIME PERIOD] to figure out.
```

**Why it works:** Breaks the passive scroll behavior with a direct instruction. The time investment ("took me 6 months") signals high value compressed into a short video.

---

## 3. The Counter-Intuitive

**Retention trigger:** Cognitive dissonance

```
The worst advice I ever followed was "[COMMON ADVICE]."
Here's what actually works instead.
```

**Why it works:** Challenges something the viewer probably believes. They stay to see if you can back it up.

---

## 4. The Proof-First

**Retention trigger:** Concrete evidence before the claim

```
[SPECIFIC RESULT -- e.g., "$12,000 in 30 days" / "10x engagement" / "500K views"].
That's not clickbait. That's what happened when I [ACTION].
Let me show you exactly how.
```

**Why it works:** Leading with the result skips the "is this worth my time?" filter. The "not clickbait" pre-emption addresses skepticism head-on.

---

## 5. The Warning

**Retention trigger:** Loss aversion

```
If you're [DOING COMMON THING], stop. Right now.
I made this mistake for [TIME PERIOD] and it cost me [SPECIFIC LOSS].
Here's what I wish someone told me.
```

**Why it works:** People are more motivated by avoiding losses than achieving gains. The personal admission makes it feel authentic, not fear-mongering.

---

## 6. The "In X Minutes"

**Retention trigger:** Clear time commitment + promised outcome

```
In the next [NUMBER] minutes, you'll understand [TOPIC] better
than [IMPRESSIVE COMPARISON -- "95% of professionals" / "people with degrees in it"].
```

**Why it works:** Low time commitment + ambitious promise. The comparison creates a benchmark the viewer wants to beat.

---

## 7. The Question Nobody Asked

**Retention trigger:** Reframing a familiar topic

```
Everyone asks "how do I [COMMON GOAL]?"
Wrong question.
The real question is "[BETTER QUESTION]" -- and the answer changes everything.
```

**Why it works:** Positions the viewer's current thinking as incomplete. The reframe creates an "aha" moment they want to complete.

---

## 8. The Time Machine

**Retention trigger:** Regret avoidance

```
A year from now, you'll wish you knew this about [TOPIC].
I'm saving you 12 months of trial and error in [VIDEO LENGTH].
```

**Why it works:** Future regret is a powerful motivator. Framing the video as time saved (not just information) raises perceived value.

---

## 9. The "I Was Wrong"

**Retention trigger:** Vulnerability + status reversal

```
I've been doing [TOPIC] for [TIME PERIOD].
And until [RECENT EVENT], I was doing it completely wrong.
Here's the thing that changed my entire approach.
```

**Why it works:** Admission of error from someone experienced creates trust and curiosity simultaneously. If an expert was wrong, the viewer definitely wants to know why.

---

## 10. The Comparison Trap

**Retention trigger:** Identity + tribal instinct

```
There are two types of people who [ACTIVITY].
One type [NEGATIVE OUTCOME]. The other [POSITIVE OUTCOME].
The difference comes down to one thing.
```

**Why it works:** Viewers instantly self-categorize and want to confirm they're in the "right" group. The "one thing" creates a clear curiosity gap.

---

## 11. The Silent Majority

**Retention trigger:** Validation + belonging

```
If you've been quietly struggling with [PROBLEM] and wondering
if you're the only one -- you're not.
Here's what [NUMBER] people figured out that changed everything.
```

**Why it works:** Validates a private frustration. The viewer feels seen, which builds immediate trust and watch time.

---

## 12. The Data Drop

**Retention trigger:** Specificity + authority

```
I analyzed [LARGE NUMBER] [DATA POINTS] over [TIME PERIOD].
[ONE SURPRISING FINDING].
Let me show you what the data actually says about [TOPIC].
```

**Why it works:** Specific numbers signal real research, not opinion. The surprising finding creates a gap the viewer needs closed.

---

## 13. The "Delete This Later"

**Retention trigger:** Urgency + scarcity of information

```
I probably shouldn't be sharing this.
But [TOPIC] has a [LOOPHOLE / HACK / METHOD] that [SPECIFIC BENEFIT],
and it won't last forever.
```

**Why it works:** Implied scarcity of the information itself. The "shouldn't be sharing" frame elevates perceived value.

---

## 14. The Speed Run

**Retention trigger:** Efficiency + challenge energy

```
I'm going to explain [COMPLEX TOPIC] faster than anyone on the internet.
Ready? [DIRECT LAUNCH INTO EXPLANATION].
```

**Why it works:** Sets a challenge energy that keeps pacing tight. The confidence signals mastery. No warm-up -- you're already learning.

---

## 15. The Uncomfortable Truth

**Retention trigger:** Emotional resonance + honesty

```
Here's the real reason you're not [DESIRED OUTCOME].
It's not [COMMON EXCUSE 1]. It's not [COMMON EXCUSE 2].
It's [HONEST, SLIGHTLY UNCOMFORTABLE TRUTH].
And fixing it is simpler than you think.
```

**Why it works:** Eliminates comfortable excuses before the viewer can reach for them. The "simpler than you think" close prevents them from disengaging out of overwhelm.

## How to Use These Hooks

### Pairing Hooks with Content

| Hook Type | Best For |
|-----------|----------|
| Insider Secret, Delete This Later | Trend content, news, exclusive info |
| Pattern Interrupt, Speed Run | Tutorial, educational |
| Counter-Intuitive, I Was Wrong | Opinion, thought leadership |
| Warning, Uncomfortable Truth | Problem-solution, advice |
| Proof-First, Data Drop | Case study, results-driven |
| Question Nobody Asked, Comparison Trap | Reframe, mindset content |
| Time Machine, Silent Majority | Motivational, community |
| In X Minutes | Any topic -- universally effective |

### Rules for Maximum Retention

1. **Deliver on the hook within 30 seconds** -- if your intro promises something, the content must follow through fast
2. **Never hook with something the video doesn't contain** -- broken promises tank your channel long-term
3. **Test 3 hooks for the same video** -- record 3 openers, pick the one that feels most natural when spoken aloud
4. **Match hook energy to content energy** -- a hype hook on a calm tutorial feels dissonant
5. **First frame matters as much as first words** -- pair the hook with a visual that reinforces the promise

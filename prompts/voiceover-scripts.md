# Voiceover Script Templates

10 plug-and-play narration scripts for YouTube Shorts, ads, explainers, and social clips. Replace `[BRACKETED]` placeholders with your content and record -- or send straight to ElevenLabs / TTS.

## 1. The Myth Buster

**Best for:** Shorts, TikTok, educational content

```
Here's something most people get wrong about [TOPIC].

They think [COMMON MISCONCEPTION]. But the data tells a different story.

[SURPRISING FACT WITH SOURCE]. That changes everything -- because it means
[REAL-WORLD IMPLICATION].

The people who understand this? They're already [POSITIVE OUTCOME].
The rest are still stuck believing [MISCONCEPTION RESTATED].

Which side are you on?
```

**Tips:** Open with confidence, not a question. The "which side" close drives comments.

## 2. The "3 Levels" Breakdown

**Best for:** Tutorial Shorts, skill-based content

```
There are 3 levels of [SKILL/TOPIC]. Most people are stuck on Level 1.

Level 1: [BEGINNER APPROACH]. This is where everyone starts.
It works, but it's slow, and you'll hit a ceiling at [SPECIFIC LIMITATION].

Level 2: [INTERMEDIATE APPROACH]. You start seeing real results here.
[SPECIFIC METRIC OR OUTCOME] becomes possible.

Level 3: [ADVANCED APPROACH]. This is where [TOP PERFORMERS] operate.
They [SPECIFIC ACTION] instead of [COMMON ACTION], and the difference is
[QUANTIFIED RESULT].

The jump from Level 2 to Level 3? It's not more effort. It's a different
way of thinking about [CORE CONCEPT].
```

**Tips:** Keep each level to 2-3 sentences max. Pacing should accelerate through the levels.

## 3. The Timeline Story

**Best for:** Personal brand, business content, transformation narratives

```
[TIME PERIOD] ago, I was [RELATABLE STARTING POINT].

I had [COMMON LIMITATION]. I thought [WRONG ASSUMPTION].

Then I discovered [KEY INSIGHT OR TOOL]. Not from a course. Not from a guru.
From [AUTHENTIC SOURCE -- trial and error / a random conversation / raw data].

Here's what changed: [SPECIFIC SHIFT IN APPROACH].

The result? [CONCRETE OUTCOME WITH NUMBERS].

I'm not telling you this to flex. I'm telling you because [REASON THIS
MATTERS TO THE VIEWER]. And if you're where I was [TIME PERIOD] ago,
[ACTIONABLE FIRST STEP].
```

**Tips:** Specificity sells. "6 months ago I had 400 subscribers" beats "a while ago I was small."

## 4. The Rapid-Fire List

**Best for:** High-retention Shorts, tool roundups, tip compilations

```
[NUMBER] [THINGS] that [DESIRABLE OUTCOME]. Let's go.

Number one: [ITEM]. [ONE-SENTENCE EXPLANATION]. Most people sleep on this.

Number two: [ITEM]. [ONE-SENTENCE EXPLANATION]. This alone [SPECIFIC RESULT].

Number three: [ITEM]. [ONE-SENTENCE EXPLANATION]. I use this [FREQUENCY].

Number four: [ITEM]. [ONE-SENTENCE EXPLANATION]. The ROI on this is [METRIC].

Number five: [ITEM]. [ONE-SENTENCE EXPLANATION]. And honestly? This one's
the reason I [OUTCOME].

Save this. You'll forget by tomorrow.
```

**Tips:** No pauses between items. The pacing should feel relentless. "Save this" CTA at the end hooks the algorithm.

## 5. The Contrarian Take

**Best for:** Thought leadership, opinion-driven content, debate starters

```
I'm about to say something that's going to make [GROUP] uncomfortable.

[POPULAR BELIEF]? It's wrong. And I can prove it.

Here's what everyone is doing: [STANDARD APPROACH]. It made sense in
[PAST ERA / OLD CONTEXT]. But we're living in [CURRENT REALITY] now.

The data shows [EVIDENCE]. [NOTABLE FIGURE OR COMPANY] already figured
this out -- they [SPECIFIC ACTION] and [RESULT].

So why is everyone still [OUTDATED BEHAVIOR]? Because [PSYCHOLOGICAL
OR STRUCTURAL REASON].

Stop following the playbook from [YEAR]. Start [NEW APPROACH].
```

**Tips:** You need real evidence for this format. Unsupported contrarian takes destroy credibility.

## 6. The "What Nobody Tells You" Reveal

**Best for:** Behind-the-scenes, industry content, creator economy

```
Nobody tells you this about [INDUSTRY / ROLE / GOAL], so I will.

The thing that actually matters isn't [OBVIOUS FACTOR].
It's [NON-OBVIOUS FACTOR].

I learned this after [SPECIFIC EXPERIENCE]. Everyone around me was focused
on [SURFACE-LEVEL METRIC]. But the people who were actually winning?
They were obsessing over [DEEPER METRIC].

Here's the difference: [CONCRETE COMPARISON].

[SURFACE METRIC] gets you [MODEST RESULT].
[DEEPER METRIC] gets you [OUTSIZED RESULT].

Next time someone tells you to focus on [SURFACE METRIC], ask them about
their [DEEPER METRIC]. Watch how fast the conversation changes.
```

**Tips:** Works best when the "hidden factor" is genuinely non-obvious. If your audience already knows it, the hook falls flat.

## 7. The Step-by-Step Tutorial

**Best for:** How-to content, tool walkthroughs, educational Shorts

```
Here's exactly how to [DESIRED OUTCOME] in [TIME/STEPS]. No fluff.

Step one: [ACTION]. Go to [SPECIFIC PLACE] and [SPECIFIC ACTION].
This takes [TIME ESTIMATE].

Step two: [ACTION]. Now [SPECIFIC INSTRUCTION]. The key here is
[CRITICAL DETAIL MOST PEOPLE MISS].

Step three: [ACTION]. This is where most people stop. Don't.
[WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU COMPLETE THIS STEP].

That's it. [SUMMARIZE THE RESULT]. The whole thing takes [TOTAL TIME]
and it's completely [FREE / ACCESSIBLE / REPEATABLE].

I use this exact process for [YOUR USE CASE].
```

**Tips:** Screen recording pairs perfectly with this script. Keep steps to 3-5 max for Shorts.

## 8. The Future Prediction

**Best for:** Trend content, tech commentary, industry analysis

```
By [FUTURE DATE], [BOLD PREDICTION].

I know that sounds [EXTREME / UNLIKELY / CRAZY]. But look at the trend line.

In [PAST YEAR], [PAST DATA POINT].
In [RECENT YEAR], [RECENT DATA POINT].
Right now, [CURRENT DATA POINT].

[COMPANY OR TECHNOLOGY] just [RECENT EVENT]. That's not a coincidence.
That's the signal.

Here's what this means for [AUDIENCE]: [PRACTICAL IMPLICATION].

The people positioning themselves now -- [SPECIFIC ACTION] -- will be
[POSITIVE OUTCOME]. Everyone else will be [NEGATIVE OUTCOME].

This isn't a prediction. It's math.
```

**Tips:** Anchor every claim in a real data point. "It's math" only lands if you showed the math.

## 9. The Comparison Breakdown

**Best for:** Product reviews, tool comparisons, "X vs Y" content

```
[OPTION A] vs [OPTION B]. Which one actually wins?

Let's break it down on the things that matter.

[CRITERION 1]: [OPTION A] gives you [SPEC]. [OPTION B] gives you [SPEC].
Winner: [OPTION] -- and it's not close.

[CRITERION 2]: Here's where it flips. [OPTION B] does [ADVANTAGE] that
[OPTION A] can't touch. If you care about [USE CASE], this matters.

[CRITERION 3]: [HONEST ASSESSMENT]. For [USER TYPE], go with [OPTION A].
For [DIFFERENT USER TYPE], [OPTION B] is the move.

[CRITERION 4 -- PRICE/VALUE]: [OPTION A] costs [PRICE]. [OPTION B] costs
[PRICE]. But the real cost is [HIDDEN FACTOR].

My take: If you [SPECIFIC NEED], pick [OPTION]. If you [DIFFERENT NEED],
pick [OPTION]. There's no universal winner -- just the right fit.
```

**Tips:** Fairness builds trust. If you clearly favor one side, your audience will discount everything you say.

## 10. The Call to Action Close

**Best for:** End of any video, sales content, community building

```
Look -- everything I just shared is free. You can screenshot it, save it,
use it today.

But here's the truth: [TOPIC] moves fast. What works this month might not
work next quarter. The people who stay ahead aren't lucky -- they have systems.

That's exactly why I built [PRODUCT/RESOURCE]. It's [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
designed for [TARGET AUDIENCE] who [DESIRED OUTCOME].

[SPECIFIC FEATURE 1]. [SPECIFIC FEATURE 2]. [SPECIFIC FEATURE 3].

If you got value from this, [PRODUCT/RESOURCE] is the next level.
Link's in the description. No pressure -- but don't sleep on it.
```

**Tips:** The "free first, paid second" structure only works if the free content genuinely delivered. Never pitch before you've proven value.

---

## Using These Scripts

1. **Pick the format** that matches your content goal
2. **Fill every placeholder** -- vague scripts produce vague voiceovers
3. **Read it aloud** before recording. If it sounds unnatural, rewrite until it flows
4. **Keep Shorts under 60 seconds** -- aim for 45-55s of spoken audio
5. **Add 0.5s pauses** after key statements to let them land
